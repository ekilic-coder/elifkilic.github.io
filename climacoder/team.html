Team

ClimaCoder is an independent research collaboration focused on heat stress and climate vulnerability in Latin America. We work outside traditional academic institutions to create open-access climate tools and analysis for regions that are often overlooked in climate research.

EK

Elif Kiliç

Environmental Engineer & Climate Scientist

I'm an environmental engineer and climate scientist focused on understanding and modeling extreme heat stress. My work involves pulling apart large climate datasets (ERA5, CMIP6), adapting physiological heat stress models to better reflect real-world conditions, and creating tools that communities can actually use to prepare for dangerous heat.

Most climate research follows funding priorities, which often means the Global South gets left behind. Through ClimaCoder, I'm building open-access heat stress maps and models for Latin America because communities shouldn't have to wait for someone else to decide their risks are worth studying.

My background is in environmental engineering and linguistics, and I hold a Master's degree from UC Berkeley where I developed the Extended Heat Index (EHI-350) for assessing occupational heat stress. When I'm not working with climate data, I'm usually traveling through Latin America or tending to my hydroponic garden.

J

Jonathan

Mathematician & Software Engineer

I'm a mathematician and software engineer who builds data pipelines and infrastructure. My background in mathematics taught me to find patterns and solve problems systematically, and software engineering gave me the tools to turn those solutions into working systems.

For ClimaCoder, I focus on the technical infrastructure: building and maintaining data pipelines, creating web-based visualization tools, and making sure our climate analysis is reproducible and accessible. Good data infrastructure isn't just about efficiency—it's about making research transparent and enabling others to build on our work.

Outside of technical work, I spend time cooking and experimenting with recipes, often using the peppers Elif grows. I believe technology should make knowledge more accessible and help people work together more effectively.

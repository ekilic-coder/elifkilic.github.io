Research Projects

I use observational data, remote sensing and climate models to study extreme heat, humidity and their health impacts. Below are examples of my recent research projects.

Heat Stress Indices in Central America

Developed spatiotemporal maps of heat stress indices (Wet Bulb Globe Temperature, Heat Index) across Central America to identify seasonal hotspots and vulnerable communities. This work highlights how extreme heat coincides with agricultural calendars and labor patterns.

Humidity and Heat Wave Co-occurrence

Analyzed historical weather records to quantify how humidity exacerbates heat waves. Results show that high dew points often coincide with temperature extremes, increasing physiological strain on outdoor workers and urban residents.

Health Impacts of Extreme Heat

Collaborated with public health experts to examine emergency room admissions during extreme heat events. We found a clear association between heat waves and increased cases of dehydration, heat stroke and kidney disease.

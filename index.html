ELİF KILIÇ

Research Scientist

I am a Research Scientist at the India Energy & Climate Center (IECC) at the University of California, Berkeley. I hold a Master's degree in Environmental Engineering from UC Berkeley, where I developed the Extended Heat Index (EHI-350) metric to better measure extreme heat stress impacts on human health and labor productivity.

My current work focuses on understanding and addressing extreme heat vulnerability across diverse communities and geographies.

Current Research

Extended Heat Index for India

India Energy & Climate Center (IECC), UC Berkeley

Developing and applying the Extended Heat Index (EHI-350) framework to assess occupational heat stress risks across Indian districts. This work combines physiological heat stress modeling with climate data to provide actionable guidance for policymakers and public health officials on protecting workers and vulnerable populations from extreme heat.

Systematic Review: Heat Stress in Latin America

ClimaCoder

Conducting a comprehensive systematic review of heat stress research and interventions across Latin America. This collaborative project synthesizes existing evidence on heat vulnerability, health impacts, and adaptation strategies in Central American and Latin American communities to identify knowledge gaps and inform future research priorities.

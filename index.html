Hi, I’m Elif.

Climate scientist & heat-stress detective turning messy weather data into tools that protect people — with a little Latin jazz, trumpet 🎺 & euphonium, DIY, and plants 🪴 sprinkled in.

I dig into extreme heat and humidity: when it happens, why it’s getting worse, and how it hits the communities most at risk. My days are a mix of coding 💻, climate models 📊, and field-ready metrics like extended heat index — all aimed at making heat risk easier to understand and act on.

Off the clock, I’m playing Latin jazz (trumpet & euphonium), hitting the dance floor 💃 (salsa, bachata, zouk), and making way too many “quick” trips to the hardware store 🛠️ for DIY fixes, plant adoptions, or my ongoing battle with the ants 🐜 that think they pay rent.

This site brings it all together: my research projects, writing on heat stress and vulnerability across Central America & Mexico, and collabs with my boyfriend over at ClimaCoder 🌍. Dive in for science 🔬, maps 🗺️, code 🧩, and stories that make climate risk feel human and solvable.

Hi! I’m Elif — a climate scientist, heat-stress detective, and unapologetic weather nerd 🌦️ based in sunny California. I spend my days figuring out how extreme heat and humidity happen, why they’re getting worse, and how they affect the people most at risk. My work blends coding 💻, climate models 📊, and a lot of late-night coffee ☕ to turn messy data into tools and stories that can help protect communities.

Outside the lab, you’re more likely to find me behind a trumpet 🎺 or euphonium 🎶 playing Latin jazz, or on a dance floor 💃 moving through salsa, bachata, or zouk. If I’m not making music, I’m probably on my bike 🚲 headed to a hardware store 🛠️ for yet another “quick” DIY supply run — whether it’s for a home project 🔧, a new plant 🪴 for my ever-growing indoor jungle, or my ongoing battle to outsmart the ants 🐜 that think they pay rent.

This site is where it all comes together: my research projects from grad school and beyond, collaborations with my boyfriend ❤️ over at ClimaCoder 🌍, and deep-dive writing ✍️ on heat stress and vulnerability across Central America and Mexico. Whether you’re here for the science 🔬, the maps 🗺️, the code 🧩, or just to peek into the day-to-day life of a climate scientist who occasionally swaps climate data for clave rhythms — welcome! I’m glad you stopped by.
